# Solution 1: Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

---

# Objective

The first approach considered was implementing **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** to strengthen authentication between the **Ground Control Station (GCS)** and the **UAV** before any communication was established.

---

# Why We Considered 2FA

During our initial study of **MAVLink**, we observed that unauthorized users could potentially establish communication with the UAV if they had access to the network. To reduce this risk, we explored adding an additional authentication step before allowing mission commands or control messages.

We selected **2FA** because it is a widely adopted security mechanism that:

- Verifies the identity of the operator.
- Reduces the risk of unauthorized access.
- Is simple to understand and implement.
- Is commonly used in modern secure systems.

Our expectation was that authenticating the operator with a second verification factor would significantly improve the overall security of UAV communication.

---

# Proposed Approach

The proposed solution required the operator to complete a **second authentication step** before the Ground Control Station was permitted to communicate with the UAV.

Only after successful verification would MAVLink messages be accepted for transmission.

The primary objective was to ensure that **only authenticated users** could issue flight commands.

---

# Conclusion

Although **2FA successfully strengthened user authentication**, further analysis revealed that it **did not secure the MAVLink communication channel itself**.

Once authentication was completed, MAVLink packets continued to be transmitted **without encryption or message protection**, leaving the communication vulnerable to network-based attacks.

---

# Limitations

The following limitations were identified:

- Does **not encrypt** MAVLink messages.
- Does **not provide message integrity verification**.
- Cannot prevent **Replay Attacks** after authentication.
- Cannot prevent **Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks**.
- Cannot prevent **Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks**.
- Adds additional authentication overhead without protecting the transmitted data.

---

# Final Decision

Since **2FA only verifies the user's identity** and does **not provide confidentiality, integrity, or authenticity for MAVLink messages**, it could not satisfy the security requirements of this project.

Therefore, **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) was rejected as the primary security solution**, and more robust cryptographic approaches were explored.
